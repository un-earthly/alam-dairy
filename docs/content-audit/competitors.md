## 2. Profile Audits
### Competitor 1: O'Natural (`onaturalbd`)
* **Category**: Premium B2C Organic Retailer.
* **Core Products**: Pure Deshi Cow Milk (priced around 120 BDT/Ltr), Tok Doi (Plain Sour Yogurt).
* **Strengths**: Established premium brand identity in Dhaka's high-income residential hubs. Organic marketing.
* **Weaknesses**: No automated subscription platform; relies on manual single orders. No B2B agricultural supplies or cattle. Sells at a high premium.
* **Opportunity for Alam Dairy**: Provide comparable raw purity, certified A2 milk, and fresh yogurt at a highly competitive rate (80 BDT/Ltr for milk vs. O'Natural's 120 BDT/Ltr) with the convenience of automated daily subscriptions.

### Competitor 2: Agromukam (`agromukam`)
* **Category**: B2B Marketplace Aggregator.
* **Core Products**: Cattle feeds (Yellow corn, concentrate bags, wheat bran), live cattle listings (Deshi Shahiwal, Deshi Black cows), milking machinery, and scale equipment.
* **Strengths**: Extensive catalog cataloging multiple vendors, robust SEO, digital footprint in rural livestock hubs.
* **Weaknesses**: Acts as a middleman/aggregator. Lacks direct quality control, own-herd quarantine facilities, or in-house veterinary inspection records. Live cattle transactions are decentralized.
* **Opportunity for Alam Dairy**: Alam Dairy operates its own pasture, quarantine facilities, and resident veterinary program. This allows us to guarantee the health history of every head of cattle sold and control feed composition directly from fields in Manikganj.

### Competitor 3: Commercial Dairies (Aarong Dairy, Pran Dairy, Milk Vita)
* **Category**: Mass-Market Conglomerates.
* **Core Products**: UHT Milk, Pasteurized Pouch Milk, Ghee, processed sweets.
* **Strengths**: Low price point, nationwide retail distribution networks, massive advertising budgets.
* **Weaknesses**: Extensive processing, reliance on reconstituted imported milk powder, long shelf-life preservatives, lack of direct source transparency.
* **Opportunity for Alam Dairy**: Focus on the value proposition of raw purity: "milked at dawn, at your door by breakfast," returnable glass bottles, and zero powders or preservatives.

## 3. Pricing Benchmark & Positioning
Based on the comparative pricing ledger in `products-price-benchmark.csv`:

* **Fresh Milk**: Alam Dairy positions at **80 BDT/Litre**, compared to premium organic milk at **120 BDT/Litre** (O'Natural), offering a 33% cost advantage for equal purity.
* **Cattle Feed**: Alam Dairy prices balanced cattle feed at **1,200 BDT/50kg bag**, compared to wholesale aggregators who price yellow corn feed up to **36,000 BDT/ton**, ensuring local farmers receive predictable pricing without market inflation.
* **Equipment**: Single cluster milking machines are priced by Alam Dairy at **18,000 BDT** (with a sale price of 16,500 BDT), contrasting with competitor prices reaching **62,000 to 70,000 BDT** on aggregator portals, offering an accessible entry point for smallholder farmers looking to automate milking.
* **Cattle**: Live Sahiwal cows are offered by Alam Dairy at **90,000 BDT** (vet inspected and certified), compared to market aggregator listings ranging between **112,750 BDT and 184,800 BDT** for uncertified stock.
