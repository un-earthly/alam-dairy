# Copywriting Style & Formatting Guidelines — AI Content Generation

This document defines the formatting, structural, and stylistic rules that frontier AI writing models must follow to produce publication-ready content for **Alam Dairy**.

---

## 1. Tone, Voice, & Perspective
* **Perspective**: Write from the perspective of a multi-generational farming family. Use collective pronouns like *"we"*, *"our"*, and *"our farm"*. Do not speak as a disconnected corporation or third-party compiler.
* **Tone**: Keep it warm, grounded, transparent, and authoritative. Avoid marketing puffery, overly dramatic adjectives, or sales clichés.
* **Target Reading Level**: Grade 7 to 8. Keep vocabulary clear and accessible for both native English readers and bilingual Bengali audiences.

---

## 2. Formatting & Markdown Standards
* **Heading Sequences**: Nest headings sequentially (`# H1` -> `## H2` -> `### H3`). Do not skip heading levels.
* **Paragraph Structure**: Limit paragraphs to a maximum of **3 to 4 sentences** (or 60-80 words). Short paragraphs improve readability on mobile devices.
* **Sentence Length**: Keep sentences short. Try to limit them to **15 to 20 words**. Avoid complex, nested sentences.
* **Tables**: Use Markdown tables for technical specifications (fat content, weight, feed analysis). Do not list raw specs as dense text blocks.
* **Callouts (Blockquotes)**: Use blockquotes for founder notes, veterinarian statements, or environmental quotes:
  > "A farm is not a factory. It is a family that happens to have four hundred hooves."
  > ── Md. Alam, Founder

---

## 3. SEO Integration & Placement
* **Keyword Density**: Keep density natural, between **1.0% and 1.5%**. Do not stuff keywords.
* **Keyword Placement**: Place the primary keyword in:
  1. The page's H1 title.
  2. One H2 subheading.
  3. The first 100 words of introductory body copy.
* **Internal Linking**: Hyperlink product mentions using absolute file links or valid relative routing slugs (e.g., linking [A2 Milk](file:///Volumes/D/projects/alam-dairy/docs/content-audit/products.md#a-dairy-products-dairy) inside herd story articles).

---

## 4. Trust-Building & E-E-A-T Standards
* **Experience**: Highlight direct farming practices (such as Napier grass feeding, April silage pits, and automated milking lines).
* **Expertise**: Include resident veterinarian inspections and veterinary vaccination cards.
* **Authoritativeness**: Reference regulatory approvals: BSTI licenses, Islamic Foundation Halal certifications, and Department of Livestock Services compliance.
* **Trustworthiness**: Provide clear, transparent pricing and address locations. Do not hide fees or make unverified medical claims about A2 milk.

---

## 5. Local Context & Terminology
* **Regional Spelling**: Use standard British/International English spelling (e.g., *Litre*, *gramme*, *organise*, *honour*).
* **Local Currency**: Use the Taka symbol (`৳`) prefix or suffix (`BDT`) consistently.
* **Familiar Agricultural Terms**: Retain local terms like *Mishti Doi*, *Desi Ghee*, *Sahiwal breed*, *Qurbani*, *MFS*, *bKash*, and *Nagad* where they are familiar to the audience.
* **Local Addresses**: Standardize local address representations (e.g., *Madaripur, Savar, Gazipur, Manikganj, Dhaka*).
