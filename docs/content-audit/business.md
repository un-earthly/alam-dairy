# Business Specification — Alam Dairy

This document establishes the strategic, operational, and commercial framework of **Alam Dairy, Bangladesh**.

---

## 1. Company Identity & Background
* **Company Name**: Alam Dairy
* **Year of Establishment**: 2015
* **Founder**: Md. Alam
* **Headquarters**: Madaripur, Bangladesh
* **Farms**: 
  1. *Savar Home Farm* (Savar, Dhaka): Primary operations center, milking parlor, pasteurization and processing unit, and farm shop.
  2. *Gazipur Pasture* (Gazipur): 40 acres of open grazing land hosting heifers and the dedicated A2 Sahiwal herd.
  3. *Manikganj Fodder Farm* (Manikganj): Fodder cultivation (Napier grass, maize) and cattle rearing/quarantine facility.

### The Brand Story
Alam Dairy began in 2015 as a micro-farm in Madaripur with a single Sahiwal cow named *Lokkhi*. Md. Alam sold milk door to door on a bicycle. Over a decade of operations, the business scaled by integrating the second generation, adding resident veterinary services, building a cold-chain delivery fleet, and developing a digital storefront that services over 2,500 families and hundreds of fellow farmers across Bangladesh.

---

## 2. Problem Statement & Mission
### Core Problems Solved
* **For Consumers (B2C)**: Address the high prevalence of adulterated milk (containing milk powders, preservatives, or starches) in metropolitan Dhaka by delivering certified pure, raw, and fresh dairy products directly to households.
* **For Farmers (B2B)**: Provide access to verified, healthy, disease-free, and high-yielding dairy cattle (traditionally bought in highly volatile, uncertified local markets) alongside standardized feeds, veterinary supplies, and equipment.

### Mission Statement
> "Pure milk is a promise, not a product."
Our mission is to provide unadulterated, farm-fresh dairy to households while empowering Bangladesh’s dairy farmers with high-quality supplies, transparent pricing, and veterinary support.

### Vision Statement
To establish the gold standard for integrated dairy farming in Bangladesh by combining traditional animal husbandry with modern cold-chain logistics, sustainable circular farming practices, and digital commerce.

---

## 3. Target Audience & Market Segmentation
### B2C Market (Dhaka Metropolitan Area)
* **Demographics**: Health-conscious urban families, parents with young children, and premium culinary consumers.
* **Target Areas**: Dhanmondi, Mirpur, Savar, and major residential sectors in Dhaka.
* **Special Sub-segments**: Individuals seeking highly digestible A2 beta-casein milk or rich buffalo milk.

### B2B Market (Nationwide)
* **Demographics**: Smallholder dairy farmers, commercial cattle ranchers, and feed/equipment distributors.
* **Target Areas**: Greater Dhaka, Savar, Gazipur, Manikganj, and rural dairy hubs across Bangladesh.

---

## 4. Value Proposition & Key Differentiators
1. **Unadulterated Traceability**: 100% pure milk sourced directly from owned herds, processed and packaged under cold-chain standards within hours of milking.
2. **Halal & Regulatory Compliance**: Products certified by the Islamic Foundation Bangladesh (Halal), BSTI (Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution), and Bangladesh Food Safety Authority.
3. **Veterinary Inspected Cattle**: All livestock sold on the platform are vet-inspected, vaccinated (FMD and anthrax), dewormed, and provided with full health records.
4. **Circularity & Sustainability**: Utilization of zero-waste agricultural loops (biogas fuel, solar-chilled storage, returnable glass bottles).

---

## 5. Commercial & Business Model
### Revenue Models
* **Direct Retail (B2C)**: High-margin transactions on consumer dairy goods.
* **Recurring Subscriptions (B2C)**: Weekly, biweekly, monthly, or daily standing orders for fresh milk and yogurt, driving predictable cash flows.
* **B2B Agricultural Supply (B2B)**: Direct sales of machinery, tools, veterinary medicines, and high-volume cattle feed.
* **Livestock Sales & Brokering (B2B)**: High-ticket direct sales of certified dairy cows, heifers, and breeding bulls via quote and inspection flows.

### Payment Methods
The checkout logic accommodates local payment options:
* **bKash** & **Nagad** (Mobile Financial Services)
* **Cash on Delivery (COD)**
* **Bank Transfer** & **Card Payments**
* **Farmer Credit (B2B)**: Standard credit limits for registered farm profiles.
